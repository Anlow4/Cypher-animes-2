Mangás

Manga is the name given to Japanese comics. It has striking features that make it different from other comic books, such as the way it is read, published, layout and features in the drawings of the characters. Many manga originated series of drawings for television, the so-called anime.

Why is it Called mangá?

It was from the creation of Hokusai that Japanese comics began to be called manga. The word is a combination of two words: man, which means involuntary, and gá, which means drawing or image.

10 Need-to-Know Things About Mangás

1

The reading order of a Japanese manga (comics) is the reverse of the western one.

2

In the manga everything is drawn by hand.

3

In Japanese, manga literally means “irresponsible drawings”.

4

Osamu Tezuka is Japan's most famous mangaka (manga artist).

5

One of the most interesting facts about manga is that its audience is mostly female.

6

In Japan, manga readers can enjoy their favorite manga along with a cup of coffee at the numerous "Manga Cafés" throughout the country.

7

In Japan, more paper is used to print manga than toilet paper.

8

In Japan, it's common, after someone doesn't want to keep a manga volume anymore, to leave it somewhere, like a table or bench, so that someone else can pick it up.

9

Each inhabitant of Japan spends around BRL 130 a year on manga.

10

One Piece, which is the most popular manga in Japan, was supposed to run for 5 years. But author Eiichiro Oda continued to create it for another 18 years and it's still being created, with no sign of stopping.
